    The Invisalign Process

    First, we’ll take impressions of your teeth and use those impressions to map out your treatment plan on our smile preview software. Then, we’ll send your plan — along with your impressions — to a lab where they’ll create your custom aligners. The aligners resemble a retainer or mouthguard, and you’ll wear each set for about two weeks before switching them out for the next ones. Each set of aligners will progressively straighten your teeth until you’ve reached your goal.

    Benefits of Clear Aligners

    • The aligners are clear, so most people won’t even notice you’re wearing them.
    • Invisalign is removable, so you can take them out to eat, brush, and floss.
    • Unlike pokey brackets and wires, the aligners are made with a smooth plastic material that won’t irritate your cheeks and gums.

    FAQs

    Invisalign candidates often have mild to moderate alignment issues. Patients with severe misalignments are usually not good candidates for the treatment. During your consultation, we’ll thoroughly examine your smile and discuss your smile goals, lifestyle, and budget to determine if Invisalign is right for you.

    When patients are finished with their treatment, we’ll usually fit them for a retainer that they’ll wear all day for the first few weeks and then transition to wearing just at night. Wearing a retainer is common practice after orthodontic treatment to keep teeth from shifting and moving out of their new positions.

    The cost of Invisalign is based on the severity of your alignment issues, your overall oral health, and more. At your consultation with Dr. Kastoori, she will walk you through the cost breakdown and discuss your payment options. One of our team members can also look into your insurance coverage and see if your orthodontic treatment is covered.
